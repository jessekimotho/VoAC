Name: John Doe
No.: A35797
Date: 31/12/1999
Time:11:50 p.m
Mode Of Death: Electrocution
I have always been a lover of words. Words have been the only thing people never succeeded in taking away from me and they are powerful in the right hands. Yet I do not intend to be pious and say that my hands have never shook at the gravity they hold. When enclosed between these daunting walls you think too much. You have way too much time on your hands to think about your sins and the endless thread of guilt it strings along. My sins have purged and purged my soul till I am just a frame of the man I used to be. Jail must be the prequel to hell.

We are allowed one letter before we die. Maybe I would have written about love if I had been kind enough to offer myself that, but that wasn't my lot in life. I don't complain though, life is a balanced diet, most of us just hate the vegetables. I don't really know what to write in my last letter. How funny! I've always been a man of many words but sometimes my pen bleeds and wrings its own soul from itself upon touching the paper. But sometimes it echoes the sound of its own silence. It's eerie, lifeless. See I'm not a consistent creator. I create a world and walk out from it, leave the content to wander in search of its own purpose. Maybe they're damned, but I fail to see how that's different from any of us.

I've always been a hard headed kid. One of the many reasons that got me here I presume. I remember my mom's mantra, "A hard head makes a soft butt." Maybe that's why she thrashed me with fire in her eyes. Only it bugged me that she seemed to enjoy it. Her demons always came out to play whenever she laid her hand on me. As a kid I never got away with anything because I could never bring my tongue to fold over backwards when required to tell a lie. Nevertheless, I never relent pursuing anything I set my heart out to do and that attitude is the right temperature for trouble to brew in this city.
A lot of things are twisted in this city. It's a city full of undertones of sin, and death has learnt to crawl stealthily as it lurks in the shadows. In this city there are no heroes, no one can afford to go against the system. This city is a wilderness of sorts only that there is no forbidden fruit. Nothing grows here because nothing touches the light and lives to see it. It's a city heavily stratified into social classes the very heart of it is wrapped in chaos. This city is toxic, it ruins anything it touches. It's like the first woman you loved, the woman who took away all your innocence, found out what your desires were, made you thirsty for them and left you high and dry tangled in the veins of your lust. You should never once think she even loved you. More than anything I've always wanted to clean up this city. Salvage whatever is left of my home. But when you live in this city long enough you learn that you can't clean up the system from the inside. Once you get into the system it corrupts you, it taints you leaving you with nothing but a charred soul. It's a city formed by its highlights and shadows never midtones.

However the good thing about this place is everyone fights for something. There is nothing as deadly as a man who wants nothing. Some fight for money, revenge, others just love the thrill; these are often the ones who die fast. But very few people are afraid of death anyway. I guess when you see too many of those around you die it becomes the norm. You get used to the cold embrace of death and it surprises you how comfortable its arms can be.&nbsp;

I never really knew what death would be like for me though. I remember fearing it but when they killed my friend, the stars shone so bright and the moon smiled at the world, I couldn't help but think it a sweet relief. People are here today and gone in a whiff.

The thing about dying men is that we're brutally honest so I wish I had a chance to talk to the younger me right now. Maybe it would have changed how things will end. I'd tell him to make his life count for we're all doomed anyway. Not to just believe in something but to stand up for it and die from it if it kills him because maybe dreams and passions makes us suicidal that way. I'd tell him to never fear opening the door when love knocks. To love deeply enough to lay himself bare in someone's hands saying here take every piece of me to get closer to you. To love till he burns in his own desire. I'd tell him to fight for those he loves. I'd tell him to laugh a lot more. I never did laugh quite enough.

Maybe I wish I had another shot at life. To get released into the world and grow old with a woman by my side. Adopt some children and have them hold my hand as I face death. But I can't. Someone somewhere much higher than me has decided that I am toxic to this city. Someone somewhere much higher than me decided that I should be identified by a number. Someone somewhere much higher than me has decided that I have to die by electricity. I always knew studying it in university would pay off!
Kind regards,
A dead man.
