Celebrants have always observed numerous well known and lesser-known New Year practices and superstitions. All of them rooting around the belief that if you engage in an event at the beggining of the year, it will set the pattern for the year to come. Therefore, when I received a hot slap with majestic capriciousness from my old man at the beggining of 2016, I was prepared for my cheeks to have unceremonious caresses and massage throught the year. That might explain why I am not much of a high-five enthusiast. I can not well predict the intentions of a high raised open palm.

<em>Beleifs: Scaring the shit outta people since the days of Sakawa the Great.</em>

Buckle up and lets take a ride to Kisii, shall we?

The folkore surrounding black cats varying from culture to culture, I was surprised to read that the Scot believe that a black cat is prosperity. Haha. Ha. Hah. No no. No way. The black cat and a Kisii witch work together. Always. Like Waiguru and NYS. You cant mention one without thinking the other. And this one I have come to beleive it's true myself. What is that you mumbled? Huh? Ooh yeah. I know. All these years of education and I haven't shaken that stupid thinking. But let me tell you, I cant see a black cat on our roof and you tell me it's going where? Shopping? To see friends? A meeting? Yeah. A meeting maybe. With it's witch master.

You see there is a story told of this mama who had the blackest cat on black Kisii. She also had a son in the States. The son invited the mama to States to stay with him. So when the son went to night shifts, he left the mother alone in the house. Na si you know how <em>zunyes</em> have cameras everywhere. So this weekend the son is on leave and the neighbor comes to him.

" We want your mother to be our trainer."

" What do you mean trainor?" Mogaka, with a puzzled look asks.

" You know, the person who teaches another pers... "

" I know the meaning of trainor, Mr Smith! What skill do you think my mother has that she should train you? "

Mr Smith goes on to show Mogaka a clip from his cctv footage.

" You see, your mother can run really well. she works out at midnight, when the world is silent. Look at that speed, very energetic, very acrobatic"
That's the story of how the mama came back home.

" Is it not the other day I was sucking mucus out of your nostrils? " She lamented as she was being driven to the airport.

" Yes, I am glad you did that. But I dont want anymore embarrassments of you running around at night naked. Go practice that back home. And besides, I can now buy a handkerchief so I need no nostril-sucking festivals."

* Drum rolls* duru du dudu du du!-Enters the Praying mantis

(I used these somewhere and someone thought it was cool. Haha. I hope it is.)

So, the praying mantis. If you have seen me you have already seen a praying mantis. Okay, body structure of course. Big head, skinny body and an eerily adult face. I have grown up to both respect and  fear this insect. Not because we look alike but because of the belief sorrounding it's existence. I ussualy think I am more of a black scorpion myself by the way.

But I digress.

We are told in our land that if you see a praying mantis  prepare your toothpicks, meat is on the way. I envied the power this insect held. The power that came with knowing whether a group of people would eat meat or not. So when I was unapologetically served hot cabbage one day after a mantis sight-seeing, I hated the ancestors for lying. They had betrayed my trust . And that is how I became skeptic of most things I heard. But if you come to think of it, some are utterly lies. Debauchery by elders.

Ati only the men are allowed to eat the back of a chicken and the gizzard. That ancestors will curse women who ate these two parts. If the man of the house is not there, the eldest son does the honors. This I believe was a scheme by the men to just eat a lot. So when one day my old man failed to make an appearance after we cooked chicken, I started early celebration.  I was singing. "Bruk off ya back" was the song on my mind allthrough. And then at the last minute when I had already called enzymes for a convocation in my mouth, an uncle showed up. Very comfortably sitting down and mama insisted he eats supper. Man! Was I livid. So that chance was gone.

"<em>Omoigwa</em> are you fine? "

" Yes, yes ofcourse <em>tata moke</em>, why wouldn't I be?"
I fake a smile. I curse him as his chocolate teeth sink into the chicken back. I mutter something under my breath to the effect of him having a bout of diarrhoea and rashes on his buttocks the whole week.

Then there is firewood. Ati there is this stone cave in a hill at my place. That when you pass there you have to throw firewood at this cave and blessings will come your way. Methinks issa lie. Yaani some old papas somewhere bila wives created this so that when you throw the firewood there they bring their lazy old selfs and take it home. Then they would light a small fire at the cave to make you believe the next morning ati spirits had discussions there about you. SMH. ( Shaking my head while waking away with all the firewood I can gather)

But others I do think are true. When bees settle in your house then wealth and prosperity is coming your way. Just check, whoever you will find has bees making a beehive inside their houses, trust me they have a prosperous family. But other beleifs were just childish, llike when you are able to step on the shadow of an eagle you will get two hundred shillings. I haven't still managed to step on one. But if I break off from your company like a madman and chase after a shadow, please understand where I come from.

What do you beleive in?
