Everyone writes about love. Writers craft their emotions into lines and proses that seek to draw the objects of their affection. They are ready to cut themselves open so their love would pour out and in a fire, consume them both. But no one ever writes about being the object of that affection. Knowing that you have the power to break the core of one's humanity. That you wear the face of Cupid and Thanatos-god of death. That you can tear apart towers so beautifully built of poetry and music. That you can kill such poor things without their resistance. No one ever writes that love can be a demon if it wills.

Yet I wish I could tell you about my exes. But they are just Cinderellas whose feet never fit in the glass shoes. Caught in the crossfire between the two warring parties my possesiveness and love are. I've left wounds that are unforgivable. Wounds only mother's are supposed to make for they know they will be there to play nurse.

Her name was Nina. The only trophy in my collection of lost lovers I am not proud of because I was not the one she loved most.

She particularly liked the foggy nights. The ones in which the air hung heavily and draped its shoulders as if gasping for the very same breath, still and pregnant with anticipation. She loved it when the fog concealed, albeit momentarily, who she had chosen to be. A night that the clouds crowd so protectively around the moon as if shielding it from the nonchalant stares of us humans, so effortlessly oblivious of its presence. A night full of so much energy yet exhibiting so little. On such nights, when even the coldest heart would ache for some warmth even though it would mean thawing. Yet change, even the slightest is uncomfortable. She marvelled at how the strong shook at the soft whispers of the wind, the tumbling finality of it all.

She stood by the lone lampost on 5th street. Propping her weight on one leg and raising the other to touch the lampost to form a perfect four. She hated her bony knees. The neon light fell gracefully on her in a way that it caressed her face and cupped it so gently in its arms unlike most men she knew who would fall for her head first, me included. Her high cheekbones, silky hair flowed so smoothly without even trying( unlike those of her counterparts that seemed to defy all the laws of gravity and stand up on their two feet as if in protest ) She chuckled at the thought.

All she needed now was one more round and she would have enough for her completion. Just one round. Snapping her out of thought was a car that halted right at her feet. He knew the drill, and so did she. In cities like this, on nights like this, in places like this where you could read the raging lust off peoples faces, how could they not?

But she had learnt to be numb. How to never get her feelings intertwined. And as he went on with his guilty pleasure, she couldn't help but stare emptily at the ceiling trying to whisk this moment away. She drowned out his moans in the sea of her flooding thoughts. She closed her eyes and waited for the weight to lift from above her. She was like a cardboard that too many nails had fit in and pulled out, leaving her with too many holes.

Standing at my door, she held a note in her hand and handed it over to me. It was as if I held her life force and it pained me to see her that way. So high and dry for a drug that tasted sweet with her since mine were not the arms that calmed her demons. I took the note as one fully aware of my vantage point in her life.

I thought of the note, crisp, almost feels holy yet for such an evil thing, the paradox in it all. I chuckled at my own wit as I pressed a puny sized sachet half filled with a white powder into her palm. Her completion.

I watched her walk to her shack, a walk punctuated by fits of running. She felt desperately the need to be complete. The need to be  in the arms that gathered her together. She like many other people was not really looking for the meaning of life but for the experience of being alive. Lost in a world that moves too fast, like one in a maze, concepts of life too subtle to grasp and too vain to clasp.

I imaginied as she sat on her couch, the same couch that I kissed her on, she burnt the powder, held her pipe and took it all in. She watched as the perplexities and worries of life merge into one sheet of complete nothingness. Nothing meant anything and she loved that. She passed out, only to resuscitate the morning after. Nina.

Some people ask me if it pained me to see a woman I love fated to be sedated in the claws of a drug. What they don't know is that she was the epitome of beauty and I understood that I was not the only one who drank from her well. My memory of her is a jigsaw puzzle of my absence, her hurt and ache I could never heal. There's no greater hurt than watching someone you love, love their drugs more than you. I can never fit the pieces of her into each other. That's why I can't let myself love another again. It's sin alright, but the devil can't be talked out of his trade. I am a collector of hearts just not this one.
