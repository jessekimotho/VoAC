<span style="font-weight:400;">Integration is hard. There's no ‘In - the - middle ‘ description to it, </span><i><span style="font-weight:400;">ati ooo…. some topics are easier</span></i><span style="font-weight:400;">….Integration is simply hard, period. <span style="font-weight:400;">For the sake of my fellow peeps who are lucky to have forgotten what integration is, allow me to jog your memory on this horrendous topic. Integration is the reverse of differentiation , usually denoted with an elongated  ‘S’ ( which sadly, doesn't stand for anything close to, sweet, sexy, or Ed Sheeran’s Shape of you). The problem with integration is that, you need to ‘unlearn’ what you learnt in differentiation…. It's like going back to square one.Ok, enough with the mathematical misery now…Let's talk about square one. </span>

<span style="font-weight:400;">Square one usually describes the starting point of a situation , the Genesis, that first action. Undoubtedly, our perspective about square one is relative to our current state. Square one could be the abode of better days, when we felt lucky,or favored or had  so much to live for ;when differentiation was easy, road trips with friends were so lit that we brought along a  fire extinguisher , when broke was a  non-existent word in our dictionary .Life was so good, the LG company could use us as  brand ambassadors . On the other hand, Square one could be the harbour of moments we wish to forget, when we felt no one deserved to see us in that state , when we had hit rock bottom, when we lost loved ones, when friends became strangers </span><i><span style="font-weight:400;">, </span></i><span style="font-weight:400;">when life seemed... vain. </span>

<span style="font-weight:400;">Regardless of these two extremities that the Square one may be to us,  reminiscing and retracing this beginning is very critical. Better days in the past are priceless memories that we are bound to cherish. Bad days in the past remind us to be  humble and grateful for the far we have come.Sometimes, going back to the beginning makes us realise mistakes that we may have overlooked before. Often, we are encouraged to forget the past and focus on the future, but then, what role would history play in our lives ? Let us keep in mind that those who fail to learn from history are doomed to repeat it. </span>

<span style="font-weight:400;">Integration is hard, but that does not mean it cannot be done.A couple of YouTube tutorials and I will be good to go ….I think.</span>
