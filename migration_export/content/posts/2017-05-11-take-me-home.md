You haven't known beauty until you've slept under the open skies.  The sky above you laid as an open book in which everything is possible that even the darkness of night doesn't hum so eerie. Once in a while fireflies puncture the darkness and the moon lies at the bottom of the blackened sky, lighting a world of nonchalant humans too carefree to notice it's presence. It stands alone in the sky like an abandoned lover with sadness in its eyes and a part of me feels one with it. For these days, sadness hunts me down, creeps around me like the bi-product of my demons and in my loneliest moments I am never alone. It is there with me.

On nights like this, I watch the stars and try to shape her eyes into constellations that only I can fathom. She is the one memory this war hasn't killed so I have countlessly sketched her face from the edges of stars and tried to paint her in stardust. On nights like this, I have used the wind as my blanket and lied awake fumbling in the dark for her scent, the scent of my meaning. I imagine how drenched in lonely the wind must be. To carry with it whispers of unfulfilled dreams, echoes of distant lovers yet never belonging anywhere. Never setting it's feet down. Never craving the soil in whose womb it was moulded.

On nights like this I think of her, as I have every night before this. Anita. She is a benign work of art. Her face was like the most beautiful sunset, toned with the magic that reminds you of home.  Her kiss, the only prayer I ever needed to know and her body, a temple I died to worship in. Anita was the woman with danger in her eyes yet it was as if the stars themselves went to sleep behind the soft gaze of her eyes. She walked like music lived in her feet and as she waltzed around everything seemed to rise to an infinite crescendo.  On nights like this I let my fantasies play with her form until my being seeks nothing but to be quenched by her. I savour every detail of her since I am afraid that they will grow hazy and she'll fade into the winds of time. After all time is cruel. I am afraid that she'll turn to smoke and mirrors so I count the days that stand between us.

On nights like this I hate the war and the enemy before me more and more. I see them shred my homeland into tatters that bear no ability to survive on their own.  I see them killing our young men as they protect our soil. I watch their innocent bodies, once loud with youthful valour, fall as silently as birds do, with so many questions of their own for the soil. Young men burnt by fires they never fueled. I see them torching my kin in churches. Turning holy places of refuge into the aftermath of ugly sins of hate. I see them rounding up women and I watch how their hands, now weapons, strangle in the dark seeking to reap anywhere but their own gardens. Leaving behind women reduced to walking like corpses, silent, hollow and numb to the happenings of life because rape is a coward that hides it's face in the make up of silence.

This war has lagged for too long but we never forget what we're fighting for. When I catch her scent in the wind I remember what I am fighting for. I fight not because I was born hating the enemy at my front but because I will die loving the one I left behind. I fight so that the women will not have to bury too much of their flesh. I fight so that my lips can rest upon hers once more as she revels in the after taste of freedom. So at the crack of dawn, when the stars have faded and the moon hidden from the light, we will wear our lives on our sleeves and advance into open hell. Caught in the crossfire between our enemies and those we left behind. The sacrificial lambs for the price of atonement and peace. But on nights like this, when the sky wears a shroud and darkness sets in, I can dream her. On nights like this, all I want is for the wind to take me home.
