<blockquote>
<h5>claustrophobia;
/ˌklɔːstrəˈfəʊbɪə/
<em>noun</em>
extreme or irrational fear of confined places.</h5>
</blockquote>
Growing up, I used to stay with my maternal grandparents because I feared my mother like hell. She would beat me up as if she was trying to kill a stray snake and that shit hurt like nonsense. I would curse her inwardly and wish for her death. Her parents were however really great folks who would pamper me like the black king I am. I used to ask my grandmother whether she gave birth to my mum while planting pepper because she was bitter as they come.

My grandfather, a local high school watchman, used to treat me like a son. He would on several occasions joke to people that I was his lastborn son, Damn I love that man. So I stayed over at my mother's home and even schooled from there till I was past the bed-wetting stage. It is, however, in this very loving environment that I developed my greatest nightmare; <strong>Claustrophobia</strong>.

Across the SDA church that we worshipped at, there lived a guy I used to hang with. Though older than me by about two elections, he considered me his closest friend. The lad had a sister my age, who was as ripe as a banana. Her hot eyes could ripen the rawest of avocados. I used to go over to their house in the pretense of visiting her brother just to have a glimpse of the lass. I indeed confirmed that God gave men two heads, but the heart cannot pump blood to both simultaneously. Blimey! I was thick as a sheep. I would rather not eat the whole day but just hang around their house, just to see her moving her voluptuous self around as I died inside. Anytime she'd talk to me I would just stand there like a stationary piece of flesh and watch her lips move as I nod like the imbecile I was. God, I was thick as hell.

Anyway, my grandpa warned me against going to their place after realizing I was spending too much time there and ignoring any other duty I was assigned to around the compound. But my other head would not listen, I would pretend to go check on the cows in the grazing field next to the church, and then crawl across the grass because the area was extremely visible from our compound, hop into the church through one window and out through another into the voluptuous lass's compound. I could feel God bury his face in his palm as I tiptoed across the church benches towards the window, wondering why he wasted precious clay on me. But is it not God who said we should love our neighbors as we love ourselves? I judged I was merely following his commandments and so everything was justified.

On this fateful day, I did a really silly thing. <em>God, I was stupid</em>. Every time I recall this particular bit I feel like the greatest embarrassment to the African race and its clients. When I got to the voluptuous lass's compound, I pretended as always to ask for her brother. She duly informed me he was not in and I could stick around and see whether he would come back soon. The lass herself was about to take a shower and said I could sit and wait for her to get done so we could talk as we waited for the brother. Her mentioning going to the shower was a great mistake because then, blood started to flow to the wrong head of the two that are gifted to men. Seeing this, she giggled and told me not to feel shy as she knew I had a crush on her and that she would fix me up once she was out of the shower - if I knew what she meant.
<blockquote>
<h3><em>I didn't know fam. </em>
<em>I did not have a clue what she meant by fixing me up, and that is when I made a very terrible assumption.</em></h3>
</blockquote>
I stripped.

<span style="color: #ff6600;">Yes I did.</span>

When she went to shower, I thought she would come back for some action. So I stripped right there in their living room and only left my "cowboy" innerwear and sat waiting for her. When she came back and opened the door, guess who was standing right there next to her?
<h5>Ooh God, God, God!</h5>
Her brother was standing there beside her, staring back at me as I did to both of them. The mental shock was tremendous. Sitting right there naked on the seat, I can't tell you what I was feeling. 'Cause I can't tell you. I just can't tell you. I can't tell you anything. I was half-dead. I was so dead that when the brother asked me what was wrong, I just lost it.

"Ants! Ants! Ants!" I shouted as I started wriggling on the seat. They went out and brought some herbal leaf which they rubbed on me as I continued screaming. My clothes had to be thoroughly scrutinized to ensure no "ants" had stayed behind to torture me later. They swept the whole place to ensure "ants" were all gone. Ants I had created to salvage my situation. Let me never hear any of you losers telling this story somewhere else because it took a lot of courage to type.

As I walked home at sundown happy to have escaped so close from the two siblings, I found that the cows were untied from the grazing field. That only meant that my grandpa had not gone for watchman duties that particular day and so my turkey was roast. I resolved that if he as much tried to quarrel me about my whereabouts, I'd tell him to go kiss a porcupine if so he wished. He didn't. As in, he did not ask me where I was, and that was as strange as it was pleasant. After dinner, the old man asked me to his bedroom.
<blockquote>
<h3><em>"There are some lemons that rolled down under my bed I need you to get for me, " he said to me. </em>
<em>Peeking down, I could not see any lemons.</em>
<em>" There is nothing here, " I told him, my head still under the bed.</em>
<em>" Move further in," he beckoned.</em></h3>
</blockquote>
It was only when I heard the door getting locked behind me that I knew I had led myself to my own trap. The old man pulled out a whip from his cupboard drawer and started to work on my ass - the only thing popping from where I was kneeling under the bed. My mind went berserk as he whooped me, shouting about how he had many times warned me from staying in people's houses all day. I was trapped, not able to move to the front as my head hit the bedroom wall, or to the back where my ass was about to ignite from the whipping. I only needed to fart, and the whole place would blow up because of the charged air. In that small space, I screamed and panicked and felt life slip out of me. I struggled like a caged rat as oxygen failed me. I begged for mercy with the last words that could be formed by my failing human form. I almost wrote my will from down there with my fingers had I not lost energy.

When he finally let me free, I had to rush out to take deep breaths. The bedroom was small, and that did not help with the ventilation problem. I developed my fear of closed spaces. I would to this day rather walk from Kisii to Nairobi than be in a bus that has all its windows closed because I would think I'm going to die. If there are stairs in a building, I will take them rather than ride on those devil's instruments you people call elevators.

They say the best way to deal with your fears is by facing them.
That would be easy for me if that fear was not death.

Miss me with that.
