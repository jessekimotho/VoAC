Samburu is a large, beautiful wallpaper. The kind that you want people to know you use. The type that you continuously unlock your phone screen for in a <em>matatu</em> so other miserable passengers with Ronaldo wallpapers can see. The acacia trees making canopies on the landscape are dotted with sleeping hills in the backdrop which makes you regret why you have never thought of visiting before. The hills themselves have rough rocks sticking out like a chin that is not well shaven or a bosom of a beautiful body that is the countryside. As I set my foot in this part of the world and looked at the magnificent beauty spread ahead of me, I whispered <em>ashe</em> <em>oleng'</em>  to the Samburu god and prepared to write a letter to my men, Rao. I have made it to Canaan;<em> I don't think I'll be coming back.</em>

The only time my village ass was ever out of Bondonya, the village at the bottom of Sameta Hill in the banana republic of Kisii was when I would get sick, and the herbs that my mother planted outside our house did not work. Coming to this your capital city to undertake my high school studies was a huge move for me. It meant I had to use another language that is not Gusii for the next phase of my life. My uncultured tongue almost committed suicide while making conversation with the sophisticated kids of Nairobi. I almost died of choking in form two when I tried to pronounce the word "kebab." A moment which forced my saliva to go the wrong pipe and luckily got saved by a desk mate who hit my back so hard as if he was spanking a bull. It is in this school that I came to see a wild animal for the first time <em>live</em> <em>live</em>¸ as my folks would say. These were monkeys that roamed the school because of its bush-like setting. The next time I ever saw a wild animal was in Kenyatta University at around 10 pm of every day, when a flock of hyenas congregates outside the Nyayo hostels to shoot their shot at first-year damsels.

<a href="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Reserve.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-6078 aligncenter" src="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Reserve.jpg" alt="" width="291" height="173" /></a>

You can, therefore, imagine the excitement I had when I was in a tour vehicle on my way to Samburu Intrepids, a tented luxury camp inside the Samburu National Reserve. It is set on raised decks which overlook the Ewaso Nyiro River with its rich endowment of wildlife. Coming from the gate to Intrepids is an approximately 30Km ride and if you're lucky you'll be able to the see the Samburu special five animals, which are a breed that you cannot see anywhere else in this land. I had to jot down the names because they were so strange; the Reticulated giraffe, Grevy's zebra, gerenuk, Somali ostrich and Beisa oryx. The most peculiar name I had ever heard up until that point was Kebab, which to date sounds like a name you'd give a village dog.

On our way to the camp, we were lucky enough to meet a Grevy's zebra, which is basically a donkey that grew up in Runda instead of carrying water in the village. A donkey with expensive makeup

“The grevy's Zebra is special because of the uniqueness of its stripes," said the tour guide.

As if in sync, we pulled out phones to take snaps of it. It passed right in front of the tour vehicle swinging its buttocks as if aware that we saw it. Reminded me how I lose my natural walk when passing in front of a large group of females, it did.

"Do you have a kebab's Zebra?" I asked the tour guide

‘Huh?"

"Naah, nothing," I replied as I almost died with internal laughter.

When we got to the Intrepids camp, we were directed towards the Heritage Hotels bar area that is just along the shores of Ewaso Nyiro. The manager joked that there might be occasional interruptions by snakes from below. I have never heard of a worse joke in my life. You don't joke about snakes, the most terrible creatures of nature. I fear those things like nonsense. I'd preferably be locked in a den of lions like Daniel, and maybe God might send his angels to save me. You've never heard anywhere, not even in Greek mythology, of a person who got along with snakes. Heck, if I were a snake, I wouldn't even go near a snake. I'd practice celibacy because I can't copulate with a fellow hideous creature.

Anyway, I had to shift places closer to the bar's counter to feel safer. We were asked to order any drink from the bar, and my damsel and l did justice to that menu. I will not say which drinks they were, but they were strong enough to wade off the fear of snakes. Ooh, by the way, any expense we were incurring was catered for courtesy of Samburu Intrepids. I will not disclose the amount, but for your curiosity, if I showed up in my village with the money worth our expenses, I would start a wizardry school that would rival egoistical Hogwarts (They keep rejecting my applications!). There were a few <em>Jorocheres</em> at the hotel, who I noticed were referred to as wageni as compared to us locals, but I am not complaining.

After a few drinks, dinner was ready. I almost laughed at the thought; I was going to eat dinner. <em>Haha</em>. Look at me now haters<em>; I am eating dinner</em>. Dinner. <em>Haha</em>. Such a strange word. From the days of my mother shouting <em>shauri</em> <em>yenu</em>!, to eating <em>ugali omena</em> pale Villa Rosa Kibandaski at campus, now here I was about to devour dinner in a five-star hotel in the middle of a National Reserve next to colonizers. <em>Haha</em>. Someone slap the fuck out of me because I need to wake up from this dream. Anyway, dinner.

Dinner is not the typical meal you idiots are used to at your bedsitters, where you wash your hands at the kitchen sink and proceed to demolish your mountain of ugali with sukuma mayai, sited on a 20 liter jerrican of water. No. Here you eat like a colonizer. A man of <em>nyadhi</em>. There is a napkin that you place on your lap to prevent the food from making you dirty or something. I found that hilarious because back at my crib, my caretaker had just cut water supply to my place since I had delayed paying rent. I had developed a long-distance relationship with the shower, and therefore I was the one going to make the food dirty here. But <em>cha muhimu ni uhai.</em>

The table was laid out well with cutlery for everything. Sijui different spoons for soup, another one for dessert, another for the main course.  Strange, because back at the crib I am in possession of one big spoon that doubles as a mwiko and also cooks vegetables. It is also the same spoon that I eat with. Lakini as we said, <em>cha muhimu ni uhai</em>.  There also were forks, knives and other strange thingamajigs I began to wonder whether they had cooked an elephant for us. The scenes on that dinner table were straight out of an anatomy dissections class. I had lived to see the day my primary school insha topic came to life; <em>Siku ya kukata na shoka</em>, or was it <em>ndovu kumla mwananawe</em>. Gosh! I don't know man; my village ass might have been high. Those drinks were pretty strong. Because I have never dined like this before, I decided to follow what my damsel was doing because she grew up in the city and might know those shenanigans. I followed her cue, and I copied as perfect as I do most of my school lab reports. I did not want to embarrass myself before these fine hosts. I reckon I might have introduced myself as her when the hosts enquired. Heck, it was the drinks. Must have been. Damn free drinks.

As we ate and listened to hyenas laughing from the shores of Nyiro River and leopards purr from top of trees around us, we talked of the next day's plans which were mostly about a Safari drive. The waiters kept asking whether we were comfortable. The fuck, are we comfortable? Get out man, this is the most comfortable a Kisii ass can get. I was shown to my room, and the sight of it had me wondering why there are only five stars to rate it. Crap man, this stuff was the whole damn solar system, whatchu talkin' ‘bout five stars. The bathroom had soap and shampoos of different types and for different body parts. This was also hilarious considering as a practicing bachelor I use the same soap for my dishes, clothes, and body. Yeeea, sneer away you idiots. You don't have to eat at my place now if you visit me.

Writing is exhausting, and you people need to start paying.  I will continue with the next part of the story soon. Lemme go eat a kebab.
