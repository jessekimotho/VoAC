<span style="color: #ff6600;">If</span> you’ve never woken up to the chitter of exotic birds, then you need to start paying for me taking my time to explain to you the kind of paradise I woke up to in the Samburu National Reserve. You wanna know what is a better feeling than the sleep after a thorough beating? Waking up to the <em>chink-chink-chink-chink-chink</em> calls from the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><em>Acryllium vulturinum</em></span>, a species of the guinea fowl. I have to admit though, the bird has an ugly head but a beautiful body and voice.

&nbsp;
<blockquote>Reminded me of one of the ladies in <em>Nairobi</em> <em>diaries</em>.</blockquote>
It’s closest relative I was informed, is the white breasted Guinea fowl, and please do not start getting any stupid images from its name. You went to read that again, didn’t ya? And you wonder why this country cannot progress.

So I wake up in the morning, use different bar soaps for every body part, brush my teeth real sharp like I am going to eat at an NYS hotel and then dress up for the animal kingdom because in about an hour, we head out for a game drive! I step out of my room and walk down to the dining area for breakfast and it’s as if all the corruption money was dumped in this place. There is a chef preparing eggs <em>live live</em>. <em>Sijui</em> omelette, sunny side up, hard scrambled eggs, eggs with cheese and whatnot. There were other mini-chefs assisting folks to pack unfamiliar dishes at the buffet table. As a child, breakfast at the foot of Sameta Hill in the village of Bondonya was white porridge with distant rumors of sugar.
<blockquote>
<h5>If you were lucky enough, you would be the one to lick the Sufuria used to prepare porridge at the end, because it was <span style="color: #ff9900;"><em>sugarlier</em> </span>at the bottom.</h5>
</blockquote>
So I took a plate and approached the buffet table which was at that time full of <em>Jorochere</em>. I sent a short prayer for protection from my ancestors, because the last time and African man dined with these folks he found himself on a boat in the Atlantic Ocean, never to return home. I decided <em>if bad, bad </em>and proceeded to serve myself until I was sure everyone in the line behind me was uneasy. A warm towel was already set for me to wipe my hands with, and I have never felt more like royalty before. I felt like a Zamunda King on a trip to Wakanda, but of course you folks wouldn’t understand this because the last time you were tended to nicely, you were being circumcised

<a href="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/WhatsApp-Image-2018-05-28-at-22.40.06.jpeg"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-6093 size-full" src="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/WhatsApp-Image-2018-05-28-at-22.40.06.jpeg" alt="" width="1280" height="720" /></a>

After heavy swallowing, I escorted my stomach to the safari vehicle. By this time, the sun was about 20 minutes away from rising and we all got psyched to record it emerge from the beautiful Samburu hills in the backdrop of the reserve. As a human who absolutely abhors waking up early, I have never seen the sun come out of its nest. The closest I come is when one of you cultured high end people posts photos with a filtered Nairobi sunrise pretending to be the hardest working people in the pothole capital of Kenya.

The best thing happened that morning; as we were driving to get a sweet spot to record the rising African sun, we stumbled upon a lone lion devouring breakfast. Has your village self ever witnessed a sunrise next to a lion having its morning meal? Is it not you who was bragging that you are sun kissed on a WhatsApp status while next to dogs celebrating their mating season in your village? So yeah. Basically I experienced the most beautiful moments of my life. Afterwards, we drove around the reserve while greeting every other animal we could find while taking pictures, because how would you believe my tale anyway? Our time of visit coincided with the dry season in this locale. Hence, the Ewaso Nyiro River which flows from Mt. Kenya was uncharacteristically dry.
<h5>The animals were struggling to quench their thirst, a reminder that dry spells were not exclusively affecting campus comrades.</h5>
The elephants though being very crafty, dig the river bed with their enormous feet and suck the earth of its hidden waters with their trunk. We observed their genius work but were advised to not make noises while at it. Basically, <em>not to address the elephant in the room.</em>

<a href="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/aksop-min.jpg"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-6097 size-full" src="http://www.voiceofanafricanchild.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/aksop-min.jpg" alt="" width="1920" height="1080" /></a>

The tour guide educated us about the conflict that the local Samburu community has been having with wild animals. Occasionally, elephants and lions walk out and invade their homes but mutual efforts have ensured a peaceful co-existence. The best part? There are very rare cases of Samburu people involving themselves in poaching activities. Beautiful people those ones with beautiful faces and hearts. We finished the game drive after three hours of thorough sight-seeing, before me and my lady felt like we should have jumped off the Safari vehicle and looked for George of the jungle to help us stay longer. Also, I don’t want to write more about my great visit there because I’m a struggling writer who has been playing hide and seek with the landlord; I do not desire for you to enjoy my little joys of life with me. Gang, until my next travel, I thought I should let you know that Samburu was a bomb!
