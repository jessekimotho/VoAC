<h2>So this is how it ends.</h2>

<hr />

With the muzzle of this gun kissing my temples. The taste of my salty tears at the edges of my lips, these lips that I used to kiss you with. These lips that won't live to kiss another day. The tears are clouding my vision but ironically maintaining my unfathomable clarity of your figures on this bed. Limp, naked and cold.

Did it have to end this way? On our matrimonial bed? A gift at our wedding. I remember with unflinching clarity our smiles with palms clasped in each other. We couldn't wait. The sermon was too long for us, the reception mundane, the sun deliberately slow to set. We wanted to get lost in the passion of our bodies together, forever. Tearing each other's tops on this bed that night. This bed that you now lie cold, motionless and in the arms of another. This bed that I now sit, with my index finger on the trigger. With the muzzle kissing my temples.

I can hear voices on the ground floor. Do you hear them my love?
<blockquote>
<h5><em>Oh sorry</em> - you can't. <span style="color: #ff9900;">I forgot.</span></h5>
</blockquote>
You are lost deep in the arms of another. I can now hear their footsteps, they must have heard the shots. These machines should all be made silent you know. But they won't find me because I would have joined you. Dead, but not naked like you. And definitely not in the arms of another, like you are. I hope they will understand and forgive me. But is there really forgiveness for people like you and me? And your secret lover? Does even the devil forgive two adulterers and one murderer? The same devil that I know they will blame in tomorrow's Daily. Did it have to end like this, ineluctably caught in savage circumstances.

So this is how it ends.
On our matrimonial bed. With sheets soaked red, reeking of not your hymen but of your betrayal. I have consigned to oblivion. They are almost at the door. I cannot stay behind. Why would I stay behind with this drudgery of life? A life excruciatingly disappointing.

So this is how it ends.
With my index finger on the trigger.
Wrenching events and a sentence to the world.
I knew love, but love did not know me.
