<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Dear John,</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"dropCap":true} -->
<p class="has-drop-cap">Thank you. Thank you for the free trip to California. For taking your precious time to write me a 300 page guide on Salinas Valley, King City and even once, New York<em> (remember that time when you told me of the train carrying vegetables in ice boxes cutting across different states?)</em>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In a book’s world, the author is a demigod with the power to create, build or snuff out characters at will.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thank you for showing me your creations. For giving me such an explicit narration of how the fickle lives of your creatures intertwined – sometimes in the simplest of ways. Thank you for Cyrus Trask, and his wife who killed herself <em>(Up to now, I am still not sure if one can justify that type of suicide).</em> Thank you for the methodical and meticulous molding of Adam Trask and his brother Charles – they were to me like Jade dynasty statues, sold off at those art events where every bidder has tattoos on their faces and arm muscles and a tiger in golden chains lying at their feet. And a monkey. You can never forget the monkey – <em>Yakuza style aye?</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator {"className":"is-style-wide"} --><hr class="wp-block-separator is-style-wide" /><!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>John, of late I zone out a lot: during conversations, on walks home in the evenings, while washing my hands after visiting the offices for a short call… I space out so much, it’s almost scary. My mind escapes my body unannounced. It flies over streams, rivers, mountains, continents, and oceans to Salinas.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Most of the time, its winter over there. <br /><em>I don’t know why.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I see a lot of snowflakes falling quietly from the dull grey sky and secretly piling up on the brown earth, covering the purple azalea that had sprung up in the previous autumn. John, I see Sam Hamilton a lot. Sometimes I think it’s because he was Irish, but then again, it could be because I greatly admired his subtle intelligence and generosity. It’s rare to find an intellectual who is genuinely generous I think. There is a way in which <em>you</em> created an air around him that sweeps me head over heels.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s not only Sam that I see at such times. I see Adam Trask too. I pity him greatly for his gullibility in love. I feel deep remorse for his trial. For creating a Garden of Eden for a wife who did not even love him. It disheartens that he is so blindly in love with Catherine Amesbury that he fails to see the stark flaws in her.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Maybe that is how we all are when we fall in love with someone. We project the picture of what we want our ideal lover to be on them and forget that they are equally human, sometimes with spots so dark we would be scared to draw any closer.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I got a question for <em>you</em>, John.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Do emotional monsters actually exist?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Did you conjure Catherine Amesbury as a fictitious character, or have you, in your lifetime, come across such an evil, heinous being. It felt so... inhuman, for one to do such things as she for the sake of personal gain.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That aside, I fell in love with your narration - so soothing - it pairs well with espresso. It was a complex story, yes, but you made it work. I think it was ingenious how the Trask and the Hamilton family existed in perpetual hatred for each other yet in so many ways, they were each other's closest of friends. How would Adam Trask, for example, have come back to his senses were it not for the friendly pelting of Sam Hamilton? Such was a true friendship, a deep, brotherly kinship that left me in awe. You, John, breathed life into your story and like an ever-present companion, made them real. The idea of inter-generational curses. The essence of human life?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Is it true that at the end of our lives, we will stop and look back at all our deeds in</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>black</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>and</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>white?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That we will think of our actions as good or bad? That as we lay in our deathbed, we will wonder if the people we leave behind will think us of good or bad people. In a world where the greys wax more and more over the blacks and whites, this is an extremely scary concept.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Best,<br />S.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
